An Analysis of the Malaysia's 15th General Election

Malaysia's 15th General Election (GE15) was held on Nov 19, 2022. It was the first general election to be held after the implementation of UNDI18, which allows Malaysians who are 18 years old or above to vote in national elections. The election comes amidst a season of political turmoil (three prime ministers in four years), which followed the watershed May 2018 elections that saw the ruling coalition lose power for the first time since independence. The political landscape has made this election particularly unpredictable. The aim of my project is to give an overview of Malaysia’s 15th General Election.

This webpage is built using HTML, CSS and Javascript. It uses the vega-lite Javascript API for constructing simple and interactive visualizations. Before building the front-end, the data is downloaded for preprocessing. All data is sourced from the Malaysia General Election Website.

While the data available is relatively clean, preprocessing is still required before it can be utilized. Most of the challenges faced during this stage came from turning the data into something that can be used with ease. This involves removing unnecessary columns, transforming the data into the desired format, and translating certain data from Malay to English. Futhermore, some data must also be derived manually by performing operations on columns. Therefore, Python and its Pandas library are used extensively in this stage. Complicated visualizations that plot based on geography (e.g chloropleth charts) are also created through Python's Matplotlib and GeoPandas.

VOTER TURNOUT (GE14 VS GE15)

The graphs above show the voter turnout for the 14th and 15th general elections in Malaysia. From the graphs, we can see that after the implementation of UNDI18, the number of people who voted in the 15th election was more than the number of people who registered to vote in the 14th election.

VOTER TURNOUT IN GE15 BY PARLIAMENT

This is a choropleth map that shows that voter turnout for each parliamentary seat. This chart is chosen because it allows for more intuitive and quicker interpretation of the data. We observe that, despite having more parliamentary seats, there are overall significantly higher turnouts in West Malaysia than East Malaysia. The data is downloaded from SPR.gov.my. AGE, GENDER AND ETHNICITY OF CANDIDATES RUNNING FOR MALAYSIA’S 15TH GENERAL ELECTION

In this section, we break down every GE15 candidates by their age, gender and ethnicity. Data is downloaded directly from the spr.gov.my website. No pre-processing is done. After uploading the data onto Github, the data is vizualized through Vega.js.

It is clear to see that male candidates dominate the election landscape.

Bumiputera candidates are the clear majority, followed by Chinese candidates, Indian candidates and others.

The median age of candidates is within the 50 to 60 years old range.
